Creation of Robot Audio for Botwarz

Published on April 5, 2021

The inspiration for Botwarz came from several arena type arcade shooters from the 1980s, namely bezerk and robotron to name a few. Bezerk was the first game to incorporate digital voice. According to IMDB, it cost $1000 per word to digitise and compress the audio data. Staying true to the robot taunts in Bezerk, Botwarz also incoporates robot digital audio but with the difference of applying the same audio effects used for the voices of the cylons in battlestar galactica. An in depth video series that goes over the details of how the cylon voices were created provides background on the digitization approach. The use of a vocoder was key in creating the cylon voice, and vocoders have been used in 1980s doctor who theme creation and in a number of songs such as 'let groove tonight' by Earth, Wind and Fire.

But how to create the voices segments in the first place? there are several online text-to-speech services that help in generating and customizing voice audio (male, female, american, english, deep or high voice etc). The audio editing suite Audacity then provides the required components for appying the appropriate sound effects to voice - synthesizer tones and a vocoder plugin. But to get the exact effect settings requires lots of experimentation. You can see (or rather hear) for yourself by playing Botwarz. So go play it now!
